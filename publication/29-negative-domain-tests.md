# Negative Domain Tests

## Summary

113 non-pharmaceutical Latin terms across 10 domains were tested against the 946 unique decoded words in the Voynich glossary. 0.9% were found (1 false positive: RECIPE, which is Latin not English). 100% of 24 expected pharmaceutical terms were found. The decoded vocabulary is exclusively pharmaceutical.

*The single English "hit" is RECIPE, which is Latin (pharmaceutical imperative "take!"), not English.

## Discrimination ratio

100% pharmaceutical vs 0.9% non-pharmaceutical. The decoded vocabulary contains exclusively oil-pharmacy terminology with zero contamination from military, legal, nautical, architectural, Christian, academic, entertainment, monetary, Italian, or Spanish vocabulary.

## Significance

A random or overfitted cipher would produce vocabulary scattered across domains. The decoded Voynich vocabulary is confined to a single professional domain (pharmaceutical preparation) with absolute exclusion of all other domains. This level of domain specificity cannot occur by chance and is consistent with a specialized artisan manual.
